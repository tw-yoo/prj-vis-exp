Compositional Chart Question

A compositional chart question is a question that requires more than one step to answer. Instead of looking at a single value in the chart, you combine two or more pieces of information to figure out the result. Your task is to create compositional questions about the given chart. Compositional question examples:

  • "What is the difference between the total number of sunny days in summer months (June, July, August) and the total number of sunny days in winter months (December, January, February)?"
    To answer the question, you have to filter data by month, get a sum and get a difference of summed values..
  • "Which month has the second highest combined number of foggy and rainy days?"
    Uses: Sum (fog + rain per month) + Sort + Nth
  • "What is the difference between the average number of sunny days in the first quarter (January–March) and the average number of sunny days in the last quarter (October–December)?"
    Uses: Filter (by quarter) + Average + Difference
